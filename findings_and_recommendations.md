# Findings and Recommendations

## Findings
1. Federal workforce size shows clear growth over the reporting period, particularly in later years.
2. Department size is concentrated among a smaller number of large organizations.
3. The workforce is primarily composed of indeterminate employees.
4. Mid-career age bands make up the largest share of the workforce.
5. Workforce distribution is uneven across geography, with concentration in key locations and the National Capital Region.
6. Department-level growth is uneven, with some organizations expanding much more than others over the selected period.

## Recommendations
1. Standardize KPI definitions for workforce reporting across reporting outputs.
2. Maintain separate views for executive overview, workforce composition, and department analysis to improve usability.
3. Document source-specific limitations, especially for geography and suppressed values.
4. Use aggregate public data for strategic workforce reporting, but avoid overstating what it can support operationally.
5. Consider a future phase using additional labeled employee survey or workforce quality datasets if more people-experience insight is required.
